Nathan Balser

Nathan Balser, from Springville, UT, and holding an MFA from the University of Utah, is an associate professor of dance at BYU. Specializing in contemporary and musical theatre, he has notable Broadway credits including "Promises, Promises" and "9 to 5: The Musical." At BYU, he's known for choreographing productions such as "The Count of Monte Cristo." Outside of dance, Nathan consults at Utah's COPA and enjoys time with his family.

Liz Dibble

Liz Dibble, trained at the San Francisco Ballet School, later ventured into Contemporary Dance at the University of Utah and BYU. With an MFA from Purchase College Conservatory of Dance, she performed in New York with Trio Dance Collective and taught at notable studios. Her choreographies have graced stages like Mark Morris Dance Center and RDT Link Series. Outside dance, Liz enjoys baking and time with her family. She researches the nexus of Gaga movement with ballet.

Jeanette Geslison

Jeanette, an Assistant Professor of Dance at BYU, serves as the Artistic Director for the BYU International Folk Dance Ensemble and other programs. Originally from Odense, Denmark, she began with ballet training and continued her dance education at BYU, focusing on cultural dance styles like Hungarian, Scandinavian, Irish, and Ukrainian. With her MFA from the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee, she's collaborated in performances and workshops from Texas to Vietnam. Outside her academic pursuits, Jeanette and her family participate in traditional music events.
